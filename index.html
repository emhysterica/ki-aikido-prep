What to expect
The examiner observes not just mechanics but your ki — calmness, extension, and connection throughout.
Before you begin
Settle into one point. Take a breath. The test starts in your mind before it starts on the mat.
1 — Kumiwaza (24 techniques)
The examiner calls each technique by name. Blend and lead — never meet force with force. Both irimi and tenkan required where listed. Maintain zanshin after each throw.
2 — Jiyu waza (free techniques)
5 throws of your choice for each of 4 attacks: Yokomenuchi (left foot forward), Katatetori (left foot & arm forward), Ushirotekubitori (right foot & hand forward), and Tantotori (knife). Stay centered and lead uke rather than reacting.
3 — Bokken 1: Happo Giri
Eight-direction sword cutting kata, performed solo. Each cut expresses extended ki. Keep one point through every direction change.
4 — Jo 1
First jo kata. Relaxed grip, weight underside, ki extending through the tip of the staff on every strike and thrust.
Etiquette
Bow in with sincerity. If you make an error — reset with one point and continue. Do not apologize mid-test. Zanshin after every technique.
