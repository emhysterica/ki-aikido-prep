# Ki Aikido — 3rd Kyu Prep App
Oregon Ki Society · Shin Shin Toitsu Aikido

---

## Option A: Standalone file (use right now, no internet needed)

1. Download `index.html` to your phone
2. Open it in Safari (iPhone) or Chrome (Android)
3. In Safari: tap the Share button → "Add to Home Screen"
   In Chrome: tap ⋮ menu → "Add to Home Screen"
4. It will appear as an app icon on your home screen
5. Works fully offline — no login required

---

## Option B: Full PWA with proper home screen icon (recommended)

This takes about 10 minutes and is free. You'll use GitHub Pages.

### Step 1 — Create a free GitHub account
Go to https://github.com and sign up (free).

### Step 2 — Create a new repository
- Click the "+" icon → "New repository"
- Name it: `ki-aikido-prep`
- Set it to **Public**
- Click "Create repository"

### Step 3 — Upload your files
Click "uploading an existing file" and upload ALL of these files:
- index.html
- manifest.json
- sw.js
- icon-192.png
- icon-512.png

Click "Commit changes"

### Step 4 — Enable GitHub Pages
- Go to your repository → Settings → Pages
- Under "Source", select "Deploy from a branch"
- Choose branch: `main`, folder: `/ (root)`
- Click Save

### Step 5 — Wait 1–2 minutes, then visit your app
Your app will be live at:
`https://YOUR-USERNAME.github.io/ki-aikido-prep/`

### Step 6 — Install on your phone
- Open that URL in Safari (iPhone) or Chrome (Android)
- Safari: tap Share → "Add to Home Screen"
- Chrome: tap ⋮ → "Add to Home Screen" or "Install app"
- It will install with the icon and work fully offline!

## What's in the app

- **Techniques** — all 24 kumiwaza + jiyu waza + weapons with checkboxes and progress tracking
- **Name quiz** — flashcard drill with Ki Aikido descriptions, filterable by category, missed-card review
- **Ki principles** — all four basic principles with dojo application notes + Tohei's five principles
- **Practice log** — log sessions by date with notes
- **Test format** — step-by-step walkthrough of what to expect on test day

All data saves locally on your device.
